About

A Mancunian, Jas is a London based actor and singer. She is currently training in Musical Theatre at the Royal Academy of Music; headed by Daniel Bowling, and boasts an experienced faculty. Since September Jas' class has experienced master classes with Imelda Staunton, Claude-Michel Sch√∂nberg, Jenna Russel, Mary Hammond and Jenna Russel.

Her credits whilst training include Gussie in Merrily We Roll Along and lead vocolist in a Cabaret directed by George Hall. Further credits include Ensemble in A West End Christmas with The MAD Trust.She will graduate in the summer of 2019.

Prior to this she completed both a Bachelor's and Master's degree in Mathematics with The University of Manchester obtaining a First Class Honours and Distinction respectively and worked as a Software Engineer.

She has performed with the North West Theatre Arts Company and toured with productions ranging from Macbeth to Christmas pantomimes across the North West whilst also being a member of an Andrew Sisters Tribute Act, The Boogie Woogie Beauties.

She co-runs Blue Balloon Theatre Company acting as the Associate Director, alongside The Artistic Director Rebecca Pythian. Blue Balloon acts as a platform for actors and others wanting to develop and showcase their own work. Blue Balloon host events such as play developmental workshops, poetry and spoken word open mic' nights, multimedia charity fundraisers and public showcases of talent.

A West End Christmas

To finish the year, the Royal Academy of Music students were invited to perform at A West End Christmas directed by Dominic Shaw and Tolley Angell. The event raises money for The Make A Difference Trust, a charity that raises funds for HIV and AIDS projects.

We performed alongside West End casts such as Lion King, Phanton of the Opera and Heathers and sang White Christmas with Brian May and Kerry Ellis.

Somewhere Waiting For You

The final show at the Royal Academy of Music before we break up for Christmas was a concert, Somewhere Waiting For You that featured music composed by Richard Rogers, his daughter Mary Rogers and her son Adam Guettel.

The show was directed by Bijan Sheibani and choreographed by Chrissie Cartwright whose ideas transformed the story of each song, together with the incredible arrangements written by our Musical Director students and Musical Director of the concert, James Spilling.

Cabaret

Finished project 2, a cabaret directed by the wonderful George Hall. George is the most knowledgable indivudal I have ever met when it comes to Musical Theatre. It was a real pleasure to work on the cabaret with George the songs included were both musical theatre classics and music hall, which was both a challenge and fun. We also performed the cabaret at Denville Hall infront of some the industries golden treasures.

Master Class with Jenna Russell

Jenna Russell is an actress, known for receiving the Tony Award nomination and Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical for playing Dot in Sunday In The Park with George, on Broadway and The West End.

I was lucky enough to attend a Master Class by Jenna Russell. She spoke about her own experiences and offered up her best tips and advice when performing. It was lovely to spend time with someone, who I felt, came from a similar background to myself, and to hear her thoughts of the industry.

Merrily We Roll Along

Who said putting on a Sondheim in 4 weeks was ever going to be easy? But we did it! I loved every second of playing the lusty diva Gussy alongside my class mates who were all incredible.

It great experience to work with the director, Sam Yates who really transformed the piece with his unique but wonderful ideas.

Master Class with Imelda Staunton

Imelda Staunton is a master of the stage and screen, she has had a long and succesful career including four Olivier Awards and a BAFTA. Her notable roles include Umbridge from Harry Potter and Mama Rose in Gypsy.

I had the honour of attending a master class with Imelda and got some brilliant advice and tips she's learned along the way - particularly on the best way to learn lines!

First week at RAM

I made the big leap from being a Software Engineer to undertaking an MA in Musical Theatre at the Royal Academy of Music to finally pursue what it is I was born to do. We finished the first week at the RAM with a sing through with the whole cohort.

I sat through every song with a huge grin on my face; feeling overwhelmed and inspired by the amount of talent and uniqueness in my year! Can't wait to see what the rest of the year brings!
