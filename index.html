ABOUT

Jas is an actor from Manchester and is currently training at the Royal Academy of Music in Musical Theatre. Prior to her training she completed a bachelor's and master's degree in Mathematics from The University of Manchester obtaining a First Class Honours and Distinction respectively.

She co-runs Blue Balloon Theatre Company acting as the Associate Director alongside northern actor Rebecca Pythian, the Artist Director. Blue Balloon acts as a platform for actors and creatives wanting to develop and showcase their own work. We have hosted events ranging from play development workshops, poetry and spoken word open mic nights, multimedia charity fundraiser and public showcases of talent.

A West End Christmas

To finish the year, the Royal Academy of Music students were invited to perform at A West End Christmas directed by Dominic Shaw and Tolley Angell. The event raises money for The Make A Difference Trust, a charity that raises funds for HIV and AIDS projects.

We performed alongside West End casts such as Lion King, Phanton of the Opera and Heathers and sang White Christmas with Brian May and Kerry Ellis.

Somewhere Waiting For You

The final show at the Royal Academy of Music before we break up for Christmas was a concert, Somewhere Waiting For You that featured music composed by Richard Rogers, his daughter Mary Rogers and her son Adam Guettel.

The show was directed by Bijan Sheibani and choreographed by Chrissie Cartwright whose ideas transformed the story of each song, together with the incredible arrangements written by our Musical Director students and Musical Director of the concert, James Spilling.

Cabaret

Finished project 2, a cabaret directed by the wonderful George Hall. George is the most knowledgable indivudal I have ever met when it comes to Musical Theatre. It was a real pleasure to work on the cabaret with George the songs included were both musical theatre classics and music hall, which was both a challenge and fun. We also performed the cabaret at Denville Hall infront of some the industries golden treasures.

Master Class with Jenna Russel

Was incredible to spend time with Jenna Russel, who I felt had a similar background to myself. She gave us resassuring advice and made us laugh too!

Merrily We Roll Along

Who said putting on a Sondheim in 4 weeks was ever going to be easy? But we did it! I loved every second of playing the lusty diva Gussy alongside my class mates who were all incredible. It also a great experience to work with the director, Sam Yates who really transformed the piece with his weird but wonderful ideas.

Master Class with Imelda Staunton

Had the honour of having a master class with the one and only Imelda Staunton and got some brilliant advice - particularly on the best way to learn lines!

First week at RAM

I made the big leap from being a Software Engineer to undertaking an MA in Musical Theatre at the Royal Academy of Music to finally pursue what it is I was born to do. We finished the first week at the RAM with a sing through with the whole cohort. I sat through every song with a huge grin on my face; feeling overwhelmed and inspired by the amount of talent and uniqueness in my year! Can't wait to see what the rest of the year brings!
